Mining

Cannock has very strong ties to Mining. There are many areas in and around Cannock where you can see hints to Mining. Two locations are Hednesford and the Pit Hill. In Hednesford there is a statue of Mining Lamp and the wall surrounding the Lamp has the names of Miners and the mine they were at. The second is at the Pit Hill where a wheel that was used at the mines is now located. The second is at the Pit Hill where a wheel that was used at the mines is now located.

Unfortunately, there is now little evidence to Cannocks ties to Mining. The last mine in Cannock Chase closed in 1993. As most would guess Mining was and still is a very dangerous job. During the time the mines were open around Cannock there were many deaths among the workers but there were some workers that were regarded as Mining Heroes for their bravery down the mines.

Such as Samuel and David Booker as they received the George Medal for their attempts to rescue miners overcome by gas at Littleton Colliery in 1937.
There are many Mining Heroes who went above and beyond for their colleagues by risking their lives to save theirs.
