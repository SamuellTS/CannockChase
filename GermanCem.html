German Cemetery

German Cemetery is a major attraction to locals and to tourists, because of its strong ties to the war. In 1959 the governments of the UK and the Federal Republic of Germany made an agreement about the future care of the German Soldiers and German Civilians that were to buried here.

The German Cemetery holds the deceased from both World Wars. With an estimated total of 4929 burials, the cemetery holds 2143 from the first World War and 2786 from the second World War. The burials from the first World War were from the German Empire and the second burials from the second World War were from Nazi Germany. As to be expected there were to be a few unidentified burials, there were five unknown burials from World War 1 and ninety from World War 2.
