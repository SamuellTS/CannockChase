Shugborough Hall

Shugborough Estate has a lot to offer to the public and has a great deal of history. There are many different areas to Shugborough Hall such as Hadrian's Arch, The Obelisk and The Chinese House. Shugborough Hall is an estate that has gone through many owners, it started off being owned by the Bishops of Lichfield and then it passed through several hands until it reached the Anson family in 1624. The estate remained in the Anson family for three generations, following the death of the 4th Earl of Lichfield the estate was allocated to the National Trust and then immediately leased to Staffordshire County Council. Management was returned to the National Trust in 2016 and it is open to the public.

The majority of the estate is open to the public such as the hall, museum, kitchen garden and model farm.
