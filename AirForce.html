Air Force

Many people do not realise the connection that Cannock has to the military and the war. RAF Hednesford was constructed between 1938 and 1939, the camp was constructed by 500 men, many of whom were locals.

Although most of the buildings on the camp stood until the camp’s closure, some were knocked down, replaced, or used for a different purpose. During the war, the camp was known as the No 6 School of Technical Training, and many thousands of personnel from the RAF, the Navy’s Fleet Air Arm and the Women’s Auxiliary Air Force served here, alongside a large number of NAAFI and civilian workers.

Due to the security at the time of the war there is very little information is known about this period in the camp’s history.

After the war, the Camp was used both for demobilizing service personnel, and preparing those for overseas postings.

In October 1950, the camp became the No 11 School of Recruit Training, and approximately 81,500 Regular and National service recruits received their 8 week basic training.

The Camp consisted of 4 wings, each wing had two squadrons, A to H. These in turn held 4 flights, numbered 1 to 32, made up of 100 men each. Each wing had its own HQ, stores, NAAFI and dining hall.

The final passing out parade consisted of 130 recruits.

The Camp closed in 1956 due to cuts in government spending.
